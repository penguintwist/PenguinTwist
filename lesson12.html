Lesson 12: Arithmetic Operators

Learn how to perform mathematical calculations in Python!

📚 Introduction

Computers are excellent at doing math quickly and accurately. Python includes several arithmetic operators that let you perform mathematical calculations just like you would with a calculator.

Whether you're calculating grades, working out change from a purchase, or solving complex equations, arithmetic operators are essential tools for any programmer!

🔬 Theory

Python provides several arithmetic operators for mathematical calculations:

+
Addition
5 + 3 = 8
-
Subtraction
10 - 4 = 6
*
Multiplication
6 * 7 = 42
/
Division
15 / 3 = 5.0
**
Exponentiation
2 ** 3 = 8
%
Modulo
17 % 5 = 2

Basic Arithmetic Examples:

# Basic calculations result1 = 10 + 5 result2 = 20 - 8 result3 = 6 * 4 result4 = 15 / 3 print("Addition:", result1) print("Subtraction:", result2) print("Multiplication:", result3) print("Division:", result4)

Advanced Operators:

# Exponentiation (power) squared = 5 ** 2 # 5 to the power of 2 cubed = 3 ** 3 # 3 to the power of 3 print("5 squared:", squared) print("3 cubed:", cubed) # Modulo (remainder) remainder1 = 17 % 5 # 17 divided by 5, remainder 2 remainder2 = 10 % 3 # 10 divided by 3, remainder 1 print("17 mod 5:", remainder1) print("10 mod 3:", remainder2)

Order of Operations (BIDMAS/BODMAS/PEMDAS):

# Python follows standard math order of operations: # BIDMAS (UK): Brackets, Indices, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction # BODMAS (UK): Brackets, Orders, Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction # PEMDAS (US): Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction result1 = 2 + 3 * 4 # = 2 + 12 = 14 result2 = (2 + 3) * 4 # = 5 * 4 = 20 result3 = 2 ** 3 + 1 # = 8 + 1 = 9 result4 = 10 / 2 * 3 # = 5.0 * 3 = 15.0 print("2 + 3 * 4 =", result1) print("(2 + 3) * 4 =", result2) print("2 ** 3 + 1 =", result3) print("10 / 2 * 3 =", result4)
💡 Tip: Division (/) always returns a float (decimal number), even if the result is a whole number. Use parentheses to control the order of operations, just like in regular math!
